openMetaAnalysis: collaborative and continuous

Problem:. The use of meta-analyses in health care is hindered by analyses being outdated(1) and overlapping(2). In this example meta-analysis of vitamin D supplementation for the prevention of fractures, has been said to have more meta-analyses than trials. These problems can be avoided if authors can collaborate and continually update analyses.
 
Solution: Collaborative and continuous meta-analysis, supported by open-source, cloud development. While wikis also offer a solution, they are reaching limits in their ability to represent complex knowledge.(3) GitHub, originally developed for collaborative development of software, has been proposed as a method to publish legislative information with transparent tracking of development. Likewise, GitHub can host medical knowledge, support team science(4), and provide collaboration beyond what is possible with wikis. GitHub transparently tracks updates to meta-analyses and provides links to related, alternative, or competing, analyses. Collaboration with GitHub complements the functions of open data repositories such as DRYAD and YODA. There is no reason an open meta-anlaysis should ever go out of date. Anyone can revise it or copy it to make alternative analyses. Similar to wikigenes, authors' contributions are tracked for academic credit. We invite authors interested in vitamin D to revise and update the meta-analysis below. We hope this example will make it easier to do so.
 
Try it: Use the editor to explore revisions to this analysis and immediately see the results. Then, you may:
  • Revise this analysis or data, by copying ('forking') the meta-analysis to a new repository, then:
    • Edit the data files. Upload files to the editor and create new plots. Upload new data files and plots to your new repository. Then publish in any journal. Please cite openMetaAnalysis in your methods.
  • To revise the methods or display of openMetaAnalysis itself:
    • Edit any of the repository files for openMetaAnalysis to improve the display and method of analysis.
  • Lastly, initiate a 'pull request' for your revisions to be pulled back into the original repository.
  • Meta-analyze another topic? Copy ('fork') the example meta-analysis to a new repository. Replace data files with a completely new topic. Upload new data files to editor and create new plots. Upload new data files and plots to your new repository.
  • Click here to see the revision history of openMetaAnalysis.
This example meta-analysis (source) of trials of Vitamin D for the prevention of fractures suggests that Vitamin D can reduce the incidence of fractures when it is taken in effective doses that its serum raise the level by at least 20 nmol/L (8 ng/mL) and achieve a final level of at least 60 nmol/L (24 ng/mL).
